Saying Goodbye

« Hostels
Previous Entries
Gringos »

I am sitting in the Cuenca, Ecuador airport about to board my flight to Quito.  I am leaving Cuenca after five extremely enjoyable weeks here, and I am also leaving behind the last remnants of home living or geographic stability.  For the next five and a half months I don’t plan to be in any one location longer than one week.  In other words: my real journey starts today.

Cuenca is such a great city, and it is hard to say goodbye.  What’s harder is saying goodbye to everyone I’ve met here: my host family, my teachers, and most of all the few good friends I’ve made here over the past weeks.  As I told them last night, it’s not that I want to stay in Cuenca, because I am so excited for all the places I have plans to visit.  Rather, I just wish it were possible to bring my friends with me.

But saying goodbye is the nature of traveling; it is the nature of having no home.  It is also something that I hate to do.  Every time I’m in this position – and having worked a job that moved me and my colleagues every year or two to a new city, I’ve done this quite a few times – a large part of me just wants to slip out the backdoor and disappear.  To some people goodbyes are an important, albeit bittersweet, memory, but to me there is no sweetness at all, only a hollow emptiness.  It is too easy to attempt to either make the other person think they were more important to you than they really were or to hide the fact that you are genuinely sad to be parting ways, and either way I usually feel a tinge of insincerity from the whole experience.

I know there will be many more goodbyes over the next several months, but maybe they will get easier too.  I suppose spending less time in any one place will make it less likely that I get to know people as well as I did here in Cuenca.  But that’s a sad outlook too, because it was the human connections as much as the sights, sounds, old buildings, high mountains, rolling rivers, and many adventures that made Cuenca such a great place to start my travels.  So even though I plan to see so many amazing places in the world in very rapid succession starting today, I also wonder if there’ll be another place quite like Cuenca to me among them all.  

November 19, 2011
